# Review Checklist

## Scope

- Does the draft match the requested topic and audience?
- Does it answer the actual research question?

## Corpus adequacy

- Is there an explicit candidate pool and a screened subset?
- Is the number of full-text notes large enough for the ambition of the draft?
- Are the most important papers or surveys obviously missing?

## Coverage

- Are major method families or viewpoints missing?
- Are there obvious blind spots in datasets, evaluation, or limitations?

## Structure

- Does the outline still make sense after drafting?
- Are sections distinct, or do they overlap?
- Does the detailed report actually follow the refined outline hierarchy?
- Were important `1.1` or `1.1.1` subsections silently collapsed away?

## Section quality

- Are important subsections substantive rather than placeholder-like?
- Did thin subsections receive a repair pass or merge decision?
- Does each subsection have a visible evidence base?

## Grounding

- Is each important claim tied to a source?
- Are citations close to the claims they support?
- Are source-free claims limited to framing or transitions?

## Overclaiming

- Does the draft infer more than the sources justify?
- Are conflicting results acknowledged?

## Revision priority

Fix in this order:

1. unsupported claims
2. weak or untraceable corpus
3. missing major coverage
4. structural mismatch between outline and draft
5. thin or placeholder-like subsections
6. citation cleanup
7. style polishing
